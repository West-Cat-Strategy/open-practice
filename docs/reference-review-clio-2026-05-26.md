## Product Suite Map

| Product surface  | Product role                                                                                                                                                                     |
| ---------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Clio Manage      | Matter-centered practice management system of record for cases, contacts, deadlines, documents, communications, time, billing, payments, reporting, and firm settings.           |
| Clio Grow        | Intake and growth CRM for leads, intake forms, appointment booking, lead-source tracking, pipeline status, client conversion, and handoff into Manage.                           |
| Clio Draft       | Document automation and forms platform for templates, document sets, court forms, questionnaires, matter-data population, downloads, and e-signature collection.                 |
| Clio Accounting  | Legal accounting layer connected to Manage for books, bank feeds, operating/trust reconciliation, ledger reporting, vendors, expenses, and financial dashboards.                 |
| Clio Work        | AI legal-work workspace for cited research, document analysis, timelines, strategy, drafting, and matter-aware legal reasoning using Clio matter context where connected.        |
| Manage AI        | AI layer inside Clio Manage for operational actions such as deadline extraction, task creation, client-update drafting, file organization, draft invoices, and approval routing. |
| Clio for Clients | Client portal and mobile experience for secure messaging, shared documents, calendar visibility, bill viewing, payments, and client uploads.                                     |
| Clio Payments    | Payment collection surface for bills, trust requests, hosted payment links, online payments, payment plans, QR/tap-to-pay flows, and payment API support where enabled.          |
| Integrations/API | App ecosystem, OAuth developer applications, APIs for Manage and Grow, custom actions, regional endpoints, webhooks, payment links, and Zapier/custom automation.                |

## Product Spec Summary

A Clio-like legal practice suite, based only on Clio's public materials, has these neutral product
requirements:

1. Use matters as the primary operational container, with contacts, deadlines, documents,
   communications, time, bills, payments, reports, and permissions tied back to matter context.
2. Support a lead-to-client lifecycle: lead capture, source attribution, form-based intake,
   appointment booking, reminders, conflict review, status tracking, and conversion to active matter.
3. Provide document work from storage through generation: searchable matter files, templates,
   reusable document sets, court/form support where available, data population, sharing, and
   e-signature collection.
4. Keep client collaboration in a secure portal layer with messages, document exchange, calendar
   visibility, bills, payment actions, uploads, and mobile-friendly access.
5. Treat billing as an approval workflow: time and expense capture, draft bills, bill review,
   delivery, reminders, payment collection, write-offs/credits, and payment status.
6. Keep trust and accounting explicit: separate operating and trust funds, matter/client balances,
   trust requests, deposits, disbursements, reconciliation, ledgers, and reports.
7. Include reporting for operational, billing, productivity, and financial decisions with saved,
   filtered, exported, and scheduled views.
8. Use AI as an assistive action layer with source context and review checkpoints, especially for
   deadlines, client updates, file organization, invoice preparation, document analysis, research,
   and first-draft legal work.
9. Provide an ecosystem boundary through OAuth, scoped APIs, app integrations, webhooks, regional
   endpoints, payment links, and no-code automation paths.
10. Make security, data portability, regional hosting, support, training, and migration visible parts
    of the product experience, not hidden implementation details.

## Caveats

- Feature availability can vary by subscription plan, add-on, beta status, region, currency,
  jurisdiction, court integration, data-hosting region, and tenant configuration.
- Public product pages, help docs, and developer docs serve different purposes. Marketing pages can
  describe product direction while help docs more often describe enabled workflows.
- AI naming is time-sensitive. Clio publicly describes Manage AI as the evolution of Clio Duo, while
  also positioning Clio Work, Grow AI, and Draft AI as separate or adjacent AI surfaces.
- API details are implementation-sensitive and should be rechecked before any integration decision,
  especially OAuth scopes, Manage versus Platform developer accounts, Grow API availability,
  webhook-supported models, regional base URLs, rate limits, and payment-link constraints.
- Security and compliance statements are Clio's public claims, not Open Practice claims. Do not reuse
  them as compliance guarantees for any other system.
